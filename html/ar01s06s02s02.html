6.2.2. Ad Hoc Orders

Ad hoc orders are not scheduled by the editing coordinators, but rather by the author themselves when necessary. They are automated to the extent that the author only needs to enter the name and the market name for the order header or the name of the order step; all other attributes are filled out by ODIS Creator. Each ad hoc order header can contain multiple ad hoc order steps. This makes it possible for a topic to be structured in multiple order steps within a team or also in order steps from various teams. It is possible to get an overview of which team is working on which topics together with the scheduled orders.

Ad hoc orders serve to bring changes or new information quickly to the workshops. All editorial objects that are created or revised within an ad hoc order are published immediately after their release and are ready for translation as translation candidates.

They are therefore not suitable to be orders for information, that still may not be published after the release and should be held back until the market introduction. This is only possible with scheduled orders.
